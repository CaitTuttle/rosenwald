Introduction

Concept #1:

Todays school rankings can be predicted by a schools population and are a better measure of community affluence than a school's success.

Concept #2::

School rankings have been used to punish (to put it simply) poor, Black schools. School rankings need to be more comprehensive, and it is necessary to address structural inequities to improve school outcomes.

Concept #3:

Rosenwald schools are some of the first examples of schools that have considered the health, wellbeing, and connectedness of communities.

Our overarching question:

In what ways can public schools incorporate the holistic strategies of Rosenwald schools to redefine and improve the measurement of school success and better meet student needs? (Link to recommendations)

Takeaways

County rankings that use gross outcomes (i.e. test scores, graduation rates) are not an accurate measure of success, but rather a measure of affluence. A more comprehensive measurement is necessary.
